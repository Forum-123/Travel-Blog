Reasons to go to Australia Written by: Alice Morris (alice_123)
Updated: 07th January 2016 at 17:15

Australia is one of the most remote countries in the world and, ironically, one of the most popular places to visit, especially among backpackers and budget travelers.


The Great Barrier Reef

One of the most famous reef systems in the world, the Great Barrier Reef is world renowned for its abundance of marine life and world-class diving opportunities. When I was there, I saw turtles, sharks, vibrant coral, and beautiful fish. You can spend one day or a few diving this reef. Though everyone leaves from Cairns, leaving from Port Douglas will get you to less crowded dive spots.


Sydney

Known for its famed opera house and harbor, Sydney also boasts an incredible bridge, great parks, delicious food, lots of free stuff to do, and amazing surfing. Whether you go to Manly Beach or hang out with everyone else at Bondi, Sydney’s a place to relax in the sun and enjoy the water. Darling Harbor has a number of good restaurants and great entrainment venues, and the Chinese Garden is quite relaxing. For a night out on the town with colorful locals, there’s nothing like King’s Cross.


Western Australia

It’s truly beautiful, with its large expanses of outback and white sand beaches that stretch for miles without a soul in sight. The West is quite the opposite to East Australia, which is crowded and overbuilt. Visit Karijini National Park, the Coral Bay and the Ningaloo Reef.


Uluru

A giant round rock covering eight kilometers of land was surprising breathtaking. The wind-blown cuts throughout the rock make it look like a wave of sand climbing over the desert. The iron in the rock produces amazing shades of red and orange during sunrise and sunset.


Jungles

In Queensland, you’ll find one of the oldest continuous tropical rainforests in the world. There are great places to go hiking, tons of wildlife and birds (watch out for crocodiles, though), and some really nice rivers and swimming holes to cool off in. If you really want to get away from it all, head far north to Cape Tribulation, where it’s just you, the jungle, and some ocean.


Surfing

Surfing is ingrained into Australian culture. The best surfing is on the East Coast, and there are loads of places where you can catch a good wave. You can head to Sydney, Bondi Beach, Queensland and Noosa, which is one of the best places to surf waves for both professionals and beginners.


Beaches

With over 50,000km of coastline, there’s no way this country could exist without beautiful beaches. The ones on the East Coast are busier than the deserted beaches on Australia’s western coastline. But with so much to choose from, you’ll always find a spot to relax by yourself. My favorites include Coral Bay and Cable Beach.


Hope this article can give you some ideas to plan your next big trip to Australia!
