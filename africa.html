What to do in South Africa Written by: Forum Desai (forumd1999)
Updated: 27th June 2017 at 07:49

With safaris, moutains, an endless coastline and cities like Cape Town, South Africa is a great place to travel as it's inexpensive, has lots of work opportunities and has plenty of adventure activities to keep everyone busy. The country's rich history, natural beauty and culture makes it a hotspot with travellers from around the world.

Cape Town

Enjoy the view of the city by either hiking up Table Mountain or by taking the cablecar to the top. The best time to visit would be during sunset, where you will witness a beautiful view of the harbour, mountains and beaches. Beside is a smaller hike up Lion's Head if the hike up Table Mountain is too much for an evening hike.

Try to visit the former prison on Robben Island, where Nelson Mandela was imprisoned for 18 years. A former inmate will guide everyone around the building, from whom you will hear stories about the first black president. It is inspiring to even sit in the same cells where prisoners, who fought for their rights, were locked away.

On weekends in Hout Bay, vendors and artisans come to the Bay Harbour Market to sell their goods, which can include everything from jewellery, art, souvenirs and food. You will also see seals, seabirds and maybe even migrating whales (June - November) to whom the Hout Bay is home.


Animals

South Africa is home to many national parks: Kruger National Park, Pilanesberg National Park, Addo National Park and the St. Lucia Wetlands. You will have an opportunity to get close to the elephants, lions, leopards, rhinos and many more animals.

The African penguin is the only penguin bred on the continent. They can be seen at Boulders Beach or Betty's Bay, which are both short drives from Cape Town. You will see colonies of penguins living on the beaches, rather than in zoos.


When to go

The dry season (winter) runs from May - September. Wildlife is easier to spot as there's less vegetation around waterholes so you would see animals gather around to quench their thirst. Most days are sunny, have little to no rain and very few mosquitos. The days produce average highs of about 17'C (63'F). The parks are less crowded but the mornings and nights do get cold.

The wet season (summer) runs from October - April. The scenery is green, the country looks fresh and most of the migratory birds are around. Parts of South Africa get temperatures averaging at 25'C (77'F). National parks are more crowded due to school holidays.

Generally, temperatures are consistent throughout the year at the coast, while arid and mountainous areas see the greatest fluctuation in temperatures. Even in the Kalahari Desert, nighttime temeperatures can drop below freezing. So pack for all occasions, as sometimes you can experience all 4 seasons in one day in South Africa.
